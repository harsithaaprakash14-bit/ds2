Key Anatomical Markers: Cattle vs. Buffalo

Distinguishing cattle and buffaloes involves parsing specific morphological features. The deep CNN model looks at these specific region logs to weigh classification logits.

Horn Structure

Cattle

Varying directions (upright, outward, or polled). Usually rounded cross-section. Slimmer taper.

Buffalo

Typically sweep backwards or curl inward. Flattened, triangular cross-section. Much thicker at base.

Skin Texture & Hair

Cattle

Dense coat covering the body. Wide spectrum of colors (white, brown, black, patches). Fine skin texture.

Buffalo

Sparsely hair-covered, showing thick dark grey or black skin. High oil gland concentration for thermoregulation.

Head Profile

Cattle

Longer face with flat or concave forehead structure. Distinct muzzle lines with smaller ears.

Buffalo

Broader forehead (often convex) with a wider muzzle. Heavy, downward-hanging ears positioned below horns.

Body Build

Cattle

Slightly leaner bone structure, taller leg-to-body ratio, distinct hump in Bos Indicus breeds.

Buffalo

Massive, low-slung torso, thick barrel chest, shorter and stockier legs. Flat spine line.
